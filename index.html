Christian Ignatius Latrobe's Voyage to South Africa
1815 - 1816

In 1815 Christian Ignatius Latrobe, a Reverend of the Moravian Church in England sets off to South Africa. Send to search for new places for more missionary settlements to come. The following maps display 3 different excursions within Latrobe's voyage. Each excursion has a different degree of granularity. The first map shows the way the Albion, a brigg on which Latrobe travled. The second map zooms in on his stay in Gnadenthal (today known as Genadendal) and his explorations around the settlement. The third map shows Latrobe's actual exploration of the south african "bushland" from Genadendal all the way up to a point behind Uitenhagen.

Exploring South Africa - A Search for a new Mission

On March 5th 1816 Christian Ignatius Latrobe went of for an exploration of the so called inner lands of the colony to inspect potential places for a third missionary settlement. Acompanied by several missionaries and Khoikhoi, they travled by ochsen wagons and explored the colony up to the border of former "Caffraria".

Master Thesis Project of Sabine Hollmann, Digital Humanities, TU Dresden, 2025

This website presents the results of the master thesis "Missionsbewegungen in der Herrnhuter Brüdergemeine - Die Reise des Christian Igantius Latrobe 1815/1816." (Missionary Movements in the Moravian Church - The Journey of Christian Igantius Latrobe 1815/1816). This project was submitted as a master's thesis for the Master's programme in Digital Humanities at the TU Dresden. All sources used are available as public domain under this link.
